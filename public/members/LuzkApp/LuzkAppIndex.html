Lutzk 1915 Land Appropriations


First Supplementary Register of Names of

Foreign immigrants and their descendants, former subjects who fought the war against the Russian Empire,who owned private buildings on leased land, who at the time of the appropriations were subject to the law dated 2 February 1915.

Complaints about the untrue composition of this Register pertaining to the submission to the Governing Senate by the Volinsk Province Authorities at the monthly point in time on the day in which this register was published by the Province Administration.

Stamp:
Derjanij Archive
Valinsk District
253024 o.Luzk, st. Veteranaja 21

Luzk District, Volinsk Province

A copy of this document was obtained from the Archive in Luzk by Howard Krushel and George Maser during one of their ancestral tours through this region. It contains the record of 1,149 tenants, mostly German, whose land and property rights were terminated in the district of Luzk in 1915.

Credits: Contributing to the translation of these documents and their preparation for publication were Julia Danneker, Arvid Pohl, Kurt Arndt, Henry Broderson, Garnet Marshall, Bill Fife, and Gary Warner. John Marsch coordinated the translation project.

Notes:

  • Alphabetical order - The original document contained sequential entries within groupings arranged alphabetically in accord with Russian Cyrillic characters. For our site, we arranged the names in alphabetical order according to Roman characters. There is therefore no "C" section.

  • Transliteration - Recreating names and surnames recorded in Russian Cyrillic is a difficult task that sometimes requires a bit of guessing. For example, should a name that is transliterated precisely as Aysner be recorded as Asner or Eisner? For this reason, you MUST consider phoenetic equivalents for the name you may be searching. You may in fact want to browse over every name on the list just to ensure that you don't miss a possible equivalent. Most of the names should be readily recognizable, even with some variant in spelling.

  • H vs. G: There is no Russian Cyrillic character that is directly equivalent to H. Therefore, many places where H should be seen, you will find a G. The name recorded as Reingold should obviously be interpreted as Reinhold. Folkengagen should be Falkenhagen, and so on. If your surname of interest starts with H, be sure to also look in the G column.

  • Multiple land descriptions: Some tenants occupied more than one piece of land, sometimes even in another village. Such land might be owned by the same owner or different ones. If applicable, such data is shown in subsequent rows under the tenant name.

  • Interpreting the columns and abbreviations:
    Seq. Num. - This is the original sequential number of the entry. You will need this if you ever want to refer back to the original document to verify information.
    Register Number - This is a numbering system applied by the authorities. The use for this number is not known but it may cross-reference some other document.
    Tenant - This is the name of the tenant. Note that in some cases you can find the name of the tenants father as well. Example: Schulz, August Gotlibov = August Schulz [son] of Gottlieb .
    (w) = widow
    Location - Shows the village where the land was located.
    (c/v) = village with church
    (c) = colony or settlement
    (h) = house
    House with addition is not clearly defined. The addition may have been a barn or stables or a tool shed. Since barns or stables or sheds are not otherwise listed, they would be included as additions.
    There is no translation for "Shoyna" though granary has been used to define it. A Shoyna was actually a building divided into three parts. Two end parts were used to store sheaves of grain for threshing at a later time. The centre part was used as a threshing floor using flails. It had huge gates on each side of the building so that a load of sheaves could be driven in, unloaded, and driven out again.
    Value of Buildings -
    (R) = rubels
    (K) = kopek
    (n/i) = not indicated
    (-- or n/k) = not known
    Land Description - Column (a) shows the number of parcels of land and column (b) shows the amount.
    D = Desjatins (area); 1 D = 2.7 acres
    S = Sajen (length); 1 S = 2.13 metres or 2.3 yards
    Land Ownership - Names of the land owners are shown. In some cases, a single parcel of land might be split between several owners. In that case the amount in desjatins or sajen (D or S) is noted in front of that name.
