Database Hints

If you find a way to improve or simplify your search in the SGGEE databases, please advise the webmaster and the hint will be included on this page.

 

Combine a surname with a date

Suppose you do not know the first name of your male SCHMIDT ancestor but you have good reason to believe he was born in 1868. Searching through the hundreds of Schmidt listings would be very tedious. Instead, set the search for “Case Sensitive / Advanced”. Enter “Schmidt” in the surnames box. Enter “(1868)” in the birth date box. Enter “M” in the sex box. Do not include any quote marks but do include the brackets around the year. You will find a little more than a dozen to search through instead of hundreds.

 

Combine a surname with sex

Find all the males or females of a given surname by entering it in the surname box and entering M or F in the sex box. This will work with a “Normal Search”.

 

Combine a surname with a place

You know your HEMMINGER ancestor lived in Friedrichsdorf (also known as Solomka). Enter “Hemminger” in the surname box and “Friedrichsdorf” in the birth place box and conduct a “Normal Search”. Repeat for the marriage and death place boxes. Repeat again for Solomka. You may also want to do a “Case Sensitive / Advanced” search using “(Friedrichsdorf)”. This will ensure that you pick up ALL instances where the original entry might read “Friedrichsdorf or Solomka” or “Solomka or Friedrichsdorf”. Again, when entering the info, do not include the quote marks but do include the brackets.
