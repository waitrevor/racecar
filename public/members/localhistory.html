Community & Local History Books

About Community History Books

Many of the areas in which our ancestors settled in Canada and the USA have produced history books of the area, variously called Centennial books, Anniversary books, Jubilee books etc. which describe or tell about the original settlers in these areas.

This page is where members having copies of these books have offered to share this information with other members by offering to lookup articles for other members if a name in a book is of interest to another member.

Books Currently Available

See also this link to an outside group that has many area histories in Canada. First do a search by subject, and then when you find a book, do a search for any surname you seek.

 

How to Use This Page

The name of the book is listed along with a description of the area covered by the book, the membership number of the person owning the book and a list of the people shown in the index of the book. If you think you may be related to one or more of these individuals and would like to know for sure, you can obtain the name and address of the person willing to do lookups, from the Member Data page.

If you ask for copies of information from these books, please remember that some of the material is copyrighted. Should the owner of the book offer to send you copies of any material, please remember to reimburse them for the cost of the copies and the postage. The minimum suggested amount is $1.00 for envelope, postage and handling plus 25 cents per page photocopied.

 

Submitting Information for This Page

If you have any books not listed here and are willing to do lookups for fellow members please provided SGGEE with the name of the book, your e-mail address and, if there is one, a scanned image or clear photocopy of the name index from the book.

Advanced Search Tips

  • + after a number or letter will return all occurrences of records with one or more of the characters preceding the +. Thus Cab+ finds Cab, Cabby, and so on.
  • * after a number or letter will return all occurrences of records with zero or more of the characters preceding the *. Thus ab* finds sad, cab, sabbath, and so on.
  • ? after a number or letter indicates that the number or letter preceding the ? is optional. Thus ou?r finds color, colour, and so on.
  • . (the period) puts any character or number in its place that is found in the records searched. Thus n.t finds not, nit, nat, and so on.
  • [^c] finds any record that does not contain c (or whatever other character is inserted after the ^ inside the brackets). Thus ar[^c] finds are, art, but not arc. If you want to find all the names that begin with the letter B, type ^B in the Name blank (case sensitve search only).
  • [0-9] puts any digit in place of the bracketed item. Thus 1234[0-9] finds 12346, 12348, 12349, and so on
  • ^ finds all records that start with the letters following the ^. Thus ^war finds war, ward, and wart, but not southward, eastward, forewarn and so on.
  • $ matches the end of the record field. Thus happy$ would find Edith Happy, but not happy birthday.
  • () groups characters together. Thus (123) finds 123, 64123, 61234, etc.
  • You can also use multiple special characters if you are brave enough. Thus (123)+ finds 123, 123123, and 123123123, and so on.
