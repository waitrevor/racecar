Public Databases

SGGEE offers public access to a selection of our databases, notably VKP and Breyer Maps. This allows non-members to search and view records such as births, marriages, and deaths from selected parishes. While the public can access essential records and search functionalities, our full selection of databases are available only to members.


Access records from the Lutheran parishes in Volhynia, Kiev, and Podolia, including births, marriages, and deaths. Some records may be incomplete but provide valuable information for genealogical research.
The core of the VKP database is the Volhynian portion of the St. Petersburg Archives records index extracted over 10 years ago by many volunteers (including many SGGEE members) and now hosted in the original form on the Odessa3 website. This portion of the original data on the Odessa3 index contains BMD data for Zhitomir, Rozyszcze, and Heimtal parishes for the years 1833 to 1885, with some years missing, approximately 72,000 line items. For more information, see our Volhynia Parishes page.

Members Only

SGGEE provides exclusive access to a comprehensive collection of databases containing detailed historical records and genealogical data. Members can explore an extensive array of indexes and original documents, including birth, marriage, and death records, as well as parish-specific information. Our databases include enhanced search capabilities and detailed records extracted from various sources, including the St. Petersburg Archive and AGAD registers. This exclusive access supports in-depth research and provides valuable insights into historical family connections.

This pedigree database is a a partial compilation of the records from the Lodz Trinity Lutheran Church for the years 1826 to 1851, as originally extracted / translated by hand by Michael Radke. The database compilation into Legacy format by John Marsch is now completed.
John Marsch says the following: "The usual disclaimers apply to this file of course. Check the SOURCE FILM is the watchword. . Radke made mistakes, I have made mistakes and the original recorder(s) made them as well. If considered necessary I (John) can check the Radke translations for members upon request. Surname spelling variations are the biggest bug-a-boo. In some cases there are three or four surname differences for an individual, some are radical."

Parish Records Index (PRI) (last update February 2012 - about 368,800 names in database) SGGEE has created a searchable database of names and other vital statistics data from the indices of the original Polish and Russian civil and parish records. See Job Central for a listing of the parishes included. In some parishes the volunteers are only extracting names from the indices, while in other cases the volunteers are extracting all the data in the original record. Note also that our database includes some personal files from a few of our members, and that data contains some records from parishes that have not been entered into the Job Central listing of parishes being worked on.

Permission has now been received from the Polish Archives to show only the indices for these records on the SGGEE website. An effort is being made to convert those records that have more than just index information into the Pedigree database, but until that is done, you can continue to ask us to do a search of the master database to see if there is additional information on any names that are of interest to you. Just send an email to databases-sggee@googlegroups.com and be sure to mention that you want a specific search of the PRI database for a specific name, including the registration number and parish name.

Access archives covering the years 1900 to 1918 for Volhynia, including births, marriages, and deaths. These newly transcribed indexes are a first point of access to the extensive collection of Lutheran parish birth, marriage and death records in the Zhytomyr Archive and Rowno Archive. The Zhytomyr Archive covers the parishes of Emilchin, Heimtal, Novograd-Volhynski, Radomyschl, and Zhytomyr for selected years in the period 1900 to 1919. The Rowno Archive covers the parishes of Tutchin, Rowno and some from Kupla church. They are incomplete but nevertheless these records contain a considerable amount of useful information.

This search tool will assist in determining the location of a village in the St. Petersburg Volhynian records, especially if it has a common name like Alexandrowka which can be found in numerous locations. Since it is sorted by film and page number, it is organized in the same way as the original parish records, and outlines the route taken by the pastor in collecting the information. The results page will show your village name in conjunction with others in the immediate area, thereby giving you a precise location. NOTE that you can also use this database to find relatives in adjoining villages.
