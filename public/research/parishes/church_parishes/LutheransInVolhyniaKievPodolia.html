Lutheran Records for Volhynia & Related Regions

 

This page includes information of available microfilms, records that can be accessed through Polish Archives (contact info in our Archives Page) and those that have been digitized by Polish Archives for Internet access. If you find films for the Lutheran Church in these regions that are not listed here or if you find any errors in the listing, please let us know by contacting us at webmaster-sggee@googlegroups.com.

The charts provide a list of microfilm numbers for Lutheran parish records within Volhynia and the neighbouring provinces. This list is primarily based on the microfilms of the Church Books of the Evangelical Lutheran Consistory of St. Petersburg 1833-1885 often referred to simply as the "St. Pete records". The full set of microfilms covers a large range of western Russia from north of St. Petersburg down to the Black Sea. It also includes links to other original parish records from other archives as noted.

The information contained in these St. Pete films was copied from the original church books by the local pastors and annually forwarded to the consistorial headquarters in St. Petersburg. Modern extractions from these microfilms for the three Volhynian parishes, Zhitomir, Rozyszcze, and Heimtal, and for Kiev and Podolia have been completed by volunteers and entered into a database. This database can be searched on the SGGEE Databases Page. The Volhynian only extractions can be viewed directly in a different format at the Odessa3 site or through its search page.

The LDS has also microfilmed some original parish and Kantorate books from the Lutheran Church in Volhynia. The Kantorate books are the most local of all materials, having been kept at the local Bethaus (chapel) by the Kantor. Such material was later copied into the parish book by the Pastor and then recopied once more into the Consistory book mentioned above. These original parish records include much more detail in the marriage records than the St. Petersburg Consistory copy and data can extend up to 1899 (with some Kantorate records possibly going a few years further). These records have been placed on line by Polish Archives and are linked directly below.

In addition to these Kantorate records, some records for Rozyszcze, Wladimir-Wolynsk, and Lutzk parishes, although not available on LDS microfilms, are held by AGAD and the Warsaw Public Registrar Office and have now been posted online. They are mainly from the 1895 to 1915 era but some go all the way up to 1939. Some are full records while others are only indexes. Online records only include those that are greater than 100 years old. Further information may be available from the Archives and you can find contact information on our Archives Page.

Please note that virtually all of the films noted below contain records of births, marriages and deaths. Where they do not, there has been a notation made by the page numbers: BM indicates births and marriages only in that portion of the film; D indicates death records only, and so on.

Neudorf / Neubrow (on the Bug River) Parish Records

This is the oldest long standing German village in former Polish territory, having been settled on the east side of the Bug River in the 1600s. Though technically in Grodno Province, this settlement is often associated with Volhynia so we are reporting the availability here. The LDS show this location incorrectly as Mosisce in Lublin Province. Th LDS also incorrectly lists them as Reformed records when in fact they are Lutheran.

A portion of these original records are held in Latvian Archives. They were recently digitized and are available for view on line. To view them, first go to the Latvian State Historical Archives page. In the left column, click on "Digital Archive: Raduraksti". When you arrive at that page, you may have to switch to English using the EN at the upper right. You have to register (button, upper right) to view the records but there is no charge. The records are not indexed. After registering, you will find the Neudorf / Neubrow records under "Parishes Outside Latvian Territory".
