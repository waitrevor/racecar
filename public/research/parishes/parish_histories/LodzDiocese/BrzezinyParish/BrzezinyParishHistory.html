Brzeziny Parish History

Author: Uta Haertling - haertlin@student.uni-kassel.de (member of the AGoFF)

The Evangelical-Augsburgian parish of Brzeziny in Middle Poland

(during the German occupation between 1939-45 Brzeziny was also called Loewenstadt)

The Evangelical-Augsburgian parish of Brzeziny was founded in 1829. Before this time a priest of the Evangelical-Augsburgian parish of Ilow would travel through the region twice a year to confirm the youths in the Evangelical (German) villages. Confirmation entries and, sometimes, marriage and death entries of this time can be found in the church books of Ilow.

The following documents of this parish can be found in the federal archive (archiwum panstwowe) in Lodz:

1. Remains of the Evangelical-Augsburgian civil register of the parish of Brzeziny:

1830, 1832, 1844: births, deaths, no marriages but some documents which were required to register an intended marriage (baptism documents and similar documents) and fragments of the years 1850-1874.

2. A register of the members of the Evangelical-Augsburgian parish of Brzeziny covering the years 1828-1851. The title was translated to me as: "Distribution of obligatory church share among members of the Lutheran parish in Brzeziny".

Books:

Kneifel, Eduard: Das Kirchspiel Brzeziny, Zur 100jaehrigen Jubilaeumsfeier der evangelisch-lutherischen Kirche in Brzeziny am 10. September 1933, Brzeziny 1933.

This book contains chapters about the history of the city and the parish Brzeziny, the school of the parish in Brzeziny, Strykow, Glogowiec, Galkowek, Borowo, Zielona Gora, Zakowice, Felicjanow, Katarzynow, Leosin, Albertow and Teolin, also the former schools in Warszewice, Jasien, Marjanow, Przylek Maly, Janow, Przanowice and Mikolajow, about the daughter church in Groembach / Laznowska Wola, and also about organisations, World War I, the church anniversaries and some statistics - all written in German (old script), of course.

Kneifel, Eduard und Harry Richter: Die evangelisch-lutherische Gemeinde Brzeziny bei Lodz/Polen 1829-1945, Vierkirchen/Schwabach 1983. Revised edition of Kneifels book of 1933.

The historical information was completed up to 1945, but it is important to know that some chapters of the earlier edition are not included in this revised book. The following chapters were left out: the daughter church of Groembach / Laznowska Wola and the chapters about the schools which had stopped to exist before 1933: Warzewice, Jaschnitz/Jasien, Marianow, Przylek Maly, Janow, Przanowice and Mikolajow! Furthermore, this edtion contains only 14 pictures versus the 33 of the first edition!
